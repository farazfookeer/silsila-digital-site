03 / Curriculum sample

What's actually taught

A representative slice of modules. Every engagement is scoped to the team's existing level and the problems they're trying to solve — nothing is taught for the sake of completeness.
Foundations
How LLMs actually work
Transformers from first principles. Tokenisation, attention, training, inference cost. Demystifies the box so teams can reason about it honestly.
Foundations
Prompt engineering for working professionals
Beyond "write better prompts." Structured prompting patterns, chain-of-thought, few-shot design, when to use which technique — with finance-domain examples.
Architecture
RAG systems for regulated data
Retrieval design, chunking strategies, embedding choices, evaluation. With explicit treatment of data residency, audit trails, and PII boundaries.
Architecture
Designing agentic workflows
Tool selection, agent loops, human-in-the-loop checkpoints, failure recovery. When to use multi-agent vs single-agent. When not to use agents at all.
Practice
Evaluation & guardrails
How to actually know if your AI system is working. LLM-as-judge, deterministic evals, drift detection, red-teaming. Spectre and PersonaEngine as worked examples.
Practice
Vendor & build-vs-buy
A practical framework for AI procurement. What to ask vendors, what to ignore, how to evaluate model performance honestly, when to build instead.
Governance
AI risk frameworks in practice
EU AI Act, PRA SS1/23, DORA, ISO 42001 — as design constraints. How to translate regulatory text into product requirements your engineers can build against.
Governance
Ethics for product teams
A working vocabulary for the harder questions: when does helpful become manipulative? What does "human oversight" really require? Drawn from current academic research.
